## 2. Logo

### Primary Mark
The TEM Motorrs logo is a two-part lockup: a **shield emblem** above a **wordmark**.

- **Shield**: A pointed-base shield shape containing a stylized sun/compass icon with radiating spikes and two curved wing elements. Conveys protection, power, and direction.
- **Wordmark**: "TEM MOTORRS" set in wide-spaced, geometric sans-serif capitals beneath the shield.
- **Finish**: Both elements use a **metallic gold** treatment — gradients from warm gold to pale champagne — evoking premium craftsmanship.

### Logo Usage Rules
- Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the "T" glyph on all sides.
- The logo may appear on **deep navy/dark purple** or **white** backgrounds only.
- Never recolor the shield or wordmark — do not use flat fills; the metallic gradient is part of the brand.
- Do not rotate, stretch, or apply drop shadows to the primary mark.
- Minimum display size: 120px wide (digital), 30mm wide (print).

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Background | Deep Navy | `#140D2E` | Primary page/slide backgrounds |
| Brand Purple | Royal Violet | `#5B21B6` → `#7C3AED` | Headings, CTAs, highlighted cards |
| Accent | Bright Violet | `#8B5CF6` | Sub-headings, icon fills, borders |
| Gold | Metallic Gold | `#C9A84C` | Logo, key stat numbers, premium callouts |

### Secondary Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light Background | Off-White | `#F5F0FF` | Section backgrounds needing contrast (e.g., market slides) |
| Text | White | `#FFFFFF` | Body copy on dark backgrounds |
| Muted Text | Silver Gray | `#A09EB8` | Secondary/supporting text |
| Teal (Data) | Muted Teal | `#5B8FA8` | Data visualizations, comparison circles |

### Color Rules
- **Dark-first**: The default treatment is white text on deep navy. Light backgrounds are the exception, not the rule.
- The brand purple gradient runs from `#5B21B6` (deep) to `#7C3AED` (bright) — use for filled CTA buttons and feature highlight cards.
- Gold is reserved for the logo and top-line financial/stat callouts only. Do not use it as a general accent color.
- Never use black (`#000000`) as a background. Use deep navy instead.

## 4. Typography

### Type Scale

| Level | Style | Treatment | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display | Extra Bold, ALL CAPS | Tight tracking | Hero headlines, slide titles |
| Heading 1 | Bold, ALL CAPS | Normal tracking | Section headers |
| Heading 2 | Bold, Mixed Case | — | Card/feature titles |
| Stat/Number | Extra Bold, Oversized | — | Key metrics (₹237Cr, 300 KM, 24 patents) |
| Body | Regular | — | Descriptions, paragraph copy |
| Label/Tag | Semi-Bold, ALL CAPS, small | Wide tracking | Category labels, badges (e.g., "CO-FOUNDER & CEO") |

### Type Rules
- Section titles are always **ALL CAPS** and **white** on dark backgrounds.
- Feature/card titles use the **brand violet** (`#8B5CF6`) for emphasis on dark backgrounds, and deep navy on light backgrounds.
- Body copy is always white on dark, dark navy on light backgrounds.
- Numbers used as visual anchors (01, 02, 03…) are displayed in an **Extra Bold, oversized** treatment in brand violet.
- Never use more than two typeface weights on a single layout surface.

## 5. Iconography & UI Elements

### Section Header Icon
Each major section opens with a **circle containing a top-right arrow (↗)** rendered in brand violet outline style. This icon signals navigation, forward momentum, and innovation.

### Numbered List Style
Sequential items use **two-digit numerals (01, 02, 03…)** in an oversized Extra Bold weight, set in brand violet, functioning as both a label and a visual anchor.

### Cards
- Dark cards: deep navy fill (`#1E1545`), subtle violet or no border, `8–12px` border radius.
- Highlighted cards: solid brand violet fill, white text.
- Light cards: near-white fill, muted border, used only on light-background sections.

### Divider / Rule
A thin horizontal rule in off-white runs across the top of every slide/page, from the tagline "BUILT TO THINK!" on the left to the page number on the right. This creates a consistent framing device.

## 6. Imagery Style

- **Product photography**: Bike renders on neutral gray or gradient backgrounds. High contrast, studio-quality lighting. The vehicle should always be the visual hero.
- **Hero images**: Large-format renders with atmospheric lighting (dark studio, subtle lens flares acceptable).
- **Context photography**: Real-world urban environments, editorial/gritty style to contrast with the clean tech aesthetic.
- **Data visuals**: Line charts and circle/Venn diagrams use the brand violet and teal palette with white labels. Keep data ink minimal.
- **Patent/document imagery**: Used as credibility anchors — always framed cleanly, not as background decoration.

---

## 7. Layout Principles

- **Grid**: Three-column card grids dominate feature presentations. Two-column splits (visual left, content right or vice versa) for detailed product/team pages.
- **Whitespace**: Generous internal padding inside cards. Section-to-section breathing room is a premium signal.
- **Hierarchy**: Lead with the number or stat, follow with a bold label, then body description. Never bury the headline.
- **Accent rule**: Each section uses one and only one "pop" highlight — an oversized number, a gold stat, or a vivid violet filled card. Avoid multiple competing focal points.

## 8. Voice & Messaging

### Tone
- **Confident, not arrogant.** Lead with proof (patents, POC validation, bookings).
- **Technical, but accessible.** Explain innovations in terms of rider benefits, not just engineering specs.
- **Urgent.** The market window is open now — the copy should reflect momentum.

### Key Messages
1. The only EV 2-wheeler solving range anxiety, charging friction, and safety simultaneously.
2. 9-minute charging · 300 km range · AI-driven safety — hardware-software integrated from the ground up.
3. "Built to Think" — intelligence embedded in the vehicle, not bolted on.
